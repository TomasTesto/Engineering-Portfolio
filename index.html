Sellafield Subsea ROV

As part of a multidisciplinary engineering team, I contributed to the design, build and testing of a subsea Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) intended to operate within Sellafield's radioactive legacy storage ponds—the most challenging and hazardous environments within the UK's highly regulated nuclear industry. The project's objective was to develop a vehicle capable of navigating confined underwater environments to characterise, collect and sample radioactive waste, supporting Sellafield's ongoing decommissioning efforts.

Key Responsibilities

CAD design & modelling | Hydrodynamic analysis | Structural validation | Instrumentation integration | Electronics assembly

Key Achievements

  • Produced detailed CAD models of the ROV structure and components, translating conceptual designs into manufacturable parts
  • Performed Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) to analyse hydrodynamic performance, computing drag force, flow patterns, sediment disturbance and thrust requirements to optimise design and manoeuvrability
  • Conducted Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to validate the structural integrity of key components under operational loading conditions, ensuring a reliable design that meets safety standards within a highly regulated environment
  • Led the instrumentation and camera system integration which relays live feedback to the operator aiding efficiency and post mission analysis

Flexible Perovskite Solar Cells

This project investigated the compatibility of commercially available poly(methyl methacrylate) (PMMA) and silicone spray as encapsulant materials for flexible perovskite solar cells. Encapsulation is a critical stage in the development of perovskite solar cells, as these devices are highly sensitive to moisture and mechanical stress, both of which can significantly degrade electrical performance over time.

Key Responsibilities

Material testing | Electrical characterisation | Microstructural analysis | Data analysis & visualisation | Technical documentation

Key Achievements

  • Applied manual spray encapsulation techniques and assessed their effectiveness against mechanical fatigue through electrical performance analysis and microstructural evaluation, developing strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Utilised Ossila Solar Cell software to measure and calculate power conversion efficiency (PCE) alongside other metrics with a single coat of silicone encapsulant achieving a PCE of 13.10% and an average fill factor of 54.05%
  • Demonstrated that three coats of silicone spray encapsulation significantly improved device stability, with an encapsulated cell retaining 93% of its initial PCE over 504 hours (21 days) compared to an 88% efficiency loss in an unencapsulated control cell
  • Managed PCE data in Excel, outputting results using MATLAB to produce clear visual representations of electrical performance metrics, aiding the interpretation of results and supporting future research

Automated Spray Encapsulation System

This project involves the design and development of a CNC automated spray encapsulation system, integrating an airbrush with a computer-controlled linear stage powered by stepper motors to enable precise and repeatable encapsulant deposition on photovoltaic cells. The system represents a significant advancement from manual spray techniques, allowing for controlled parameters and systematic optimisation.

Key Responsibilities

Mechanical design (CAD) | G-code programming | Arduino microcontroller development | Parameter optimisation | Hardware integration

Key Achievements

  • Utilised Creo CAD software to design a detailed Cartesian frame guiding fabrication decisions and ensuring dimensional accuracy across all fabricated components
  • Developed and implemented G-code programming to control servo motors which power the linear stage, enabling accurate and reliable positioning of the airbrush during the encapsulation process
  • Programmed an Arduino microcontroller using C++ to control the actuation of a solenoid valve, regulating nitrogen flow to the airbrush with precise timing control
  • Currently optimising spraying parameters including flow rate, nozzle distance and transverse nozzle speed to determine the optimal deposition parameters for producing high quality, uniform encapsulation layers. This demonstrates a systematic and analytical approach to control system design transferable to decommissioning projects

NACA 7316 Aerofoil CFD Simulation

This project involved the computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulation of turbulent subsonic flow over a NACA 7316 aerofoil using SU2, an open-source high-fidelity simulation platform. A Reynolds-averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) solver was employed alongside the SST turbulence model to capture both near-wall and far-field turbulent flow behaviour across a range of angles of attack, with results validated against XFOIL reference data.

Key Responsibilities

CAD geometry creation | Mesh generation & refinement | Solver configuration | Simulation execution | Post-processing & visualisation | Technical documentation

Key Achievements

  • Configured and executed a RANS-based CFD simulation in SU2 using the SST turbulence model and JST spatial discretisation scheme, demonstrating practical experience in simulation setup and numerical methods directly applicable to engineering assessment roles
  • Validated lift coefficient (Cl) results against XFOIL software reference data across multiple angles of attack, achieving strong agreement with a simulated Cl of 1.21 compared to 1.28 at α = 4.07°, with findings formally written up demonstrating strong technical documentation and reporting skills
  • Produced Mach number and pressure distribution contour plots using Paraview software, enabling the interpretation and communication of flow behaviour, demonstrating proficiency in CFD post-processing and results visualisation
  • Applied a systematic approach throughout the simulation workflow, from the initial solver configuration through to results and validation, reflecting the rigorous methodology required in professional engineering assessment and numerical simulation work
